You get free prescriptions and dental care

Because you're pregnant or have had a baby in the last 12 months, you get free:

  • NHS prescriptions (with a valid maternity exemption certificate)
  • NHS dental treatment

You don't get help towards the cost of:

  • NHS sight tests
  • New or replacement glasses or contact lenses
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • Travel to receive NHS treatment

What you should do next

Prescriptions

To get free prescriptions you need to have a valid maternity exemption certificate. Get an application form from your midwife, doctor or health visitor.

You can apply while you're pregnant and up to a year after your baby was born.

Loss of a baby

If you lost your baby after the 24th week of your pregnancy, you can still apply for a maternity exemption certificate.

If you have a miscarriage, abortion or stillborn birth you can use your maternity exemption certificate until it expires.


Dental check ups and treatment

To get free dental care you may need to show proof of your pregnancy, or that you have had a baby in the past 12 months. You can show things like:

  • a valid maternity exemption certificate
  • a MATB1 certificate issued by your midwife or GP
