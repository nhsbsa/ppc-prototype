Based on what you've told us There are 2 ways that could help you pay for your NHS costs

Apply for help

You could be eligible for help depending on your circumstances

If you have less than £128 left at the end of the month, after you have paid your housing costs and council tax then you are likely to get help.

You don't have to be claiming benefits to apply.

Find out more

You could get help with:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental treatment
  • NHS sight tests
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • new glasses or contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • travel for NHS treatment

    • Prepay for prescriptons

    Save money on NHS prescriptions by prepaying for them

    You can pay a set price for prescriptions for 3 or 12 months, no matter how many you need.

    A prescription costs £8.60 per item, but prepayment costs:

    • £29.10 for 3 month
    • £104 for 12 months
      (or 10 payments of £10.40)
